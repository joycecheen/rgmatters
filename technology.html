The Problem

The Decades-Long Dilemma in Materials for Radiation Scintillation and Detection

The multi-billion dollar global radiation scintillation and detection market faces a decades-long dilemma: organic materials are low-cost and scalable but suffer from low sensitivity, while inorganic materials offer high sensitivity but are expensive, brittle, hydroscopic, and difficult to fabricate. Despite years of incremental advances, the industry still lacks a material solution that combines high-sensitivity, scalability, flexibility, simplicity, and affordability. This has constrained innovation in critical sectors from homeland security, nuclear safety, non-destructive testing, medical imaging, and nuclear medicine.

Our Solution: OD OMHHs

Where Organic Simplicity Meets Inorganic Performance

Our patented organic–inorganic hybrid materials, known as zero-dimensional (0D) organic metal halide hybrids (OMHHs), are designed to drive the next generation of radiation scintillators and detectors. These advanced materials uniquely combine the scalability, flexibility, simplicity, and cost-efficiency of organics with the high detection performance traditionally found only in inorganic crystals. This powerful synergy enables innovative solutions such as flexible, large-area scintillators and detectors for applications in security screening, nuclear detection, industrial inspection, and medical imaging.

In addition, our 0D OMHHs are non-toxic, lead-free, and rare-earth-free, offering a sustainable alternative that aligns with the environmental goals of modern industries and regulatory standards.
