The Problem

The Decades-Long Dilemma in Materials for Radiation Scintillation and Detection

The global radiation scintillation and detection market faces a long-standing dilemma. Organic materials are low-cost, fast responsive, and scalable but suffer from low sensitivity. In contrast, inorganic materials offer high sensitivity but are brittle, rigid, and require costly, high temperature or high vacuum processing, in addition to being often hygroscopic. Despite years of incremental advances, the industry still lacks a material solution that combines high-sensitivity, fast response, non-hygroscopicity, flexibility, and scalability through low-cost synthesis. This gap has significantly hindered innovation in critical radiation sectors, including homeland security, nuclear industry, nuclear medicine, industrial non-destructive testing, medical imaging, and high energy physics.

Our Solution: 0D OMHHs

Where Organic Simplicity Meets Inorganic Performance

Our patented organic–inorganic hybrid materials, known as zero-dimensional organic metal halide hybrids (0D-OMHHs), are designed to drive the next generation of radiation scintillators and detectors. These advanced 0D-OMHH materials uniquely combine the benefits of both organic and inorganic materials, offering a powerful synergy of high-sensitivity, fast response, non-hygroscopicity, flexibility, and scalability through low-cost synthesis. This breakthrough technology enables the creation of affordable, flexible, large-area radiation scintillators and detectors with high sensitivity and fast response. This will spur innovation in critical sectors such as homeland security, nuclear industry, nuclear medicine, industrial non-destructive testing, medical imaging, and high energy physics.

Furthermore, our 0D-OMHHs are non-toxic, lead-free, and rare-earth-free, and they are fabricated using energy-saving processing, offering a sustainable alternative that aligns with the environmental goals of modern industries and regulatory standards.
